Understanding The Problem

What is Engine Carbon Cleaning?

Carbon buildup is the accumulation of black soot and hardened fuel deposits inside your engine's combustion chamber, intake valves, fuel injectors, and exhaust system.

Every time you drive, especially in Hyderabad's stop-and-go traffic, incomplete fuel combustion leaves behind carbon deposits. Over months and years, these deposits harden like arterial plaque, choking your engine's performance.

"Like cholesterol blocks human arteries, carbon blocks engine pathways. The result is reduced airflow, improper fuel mixing, and gradual power loss."

Traditional solutions require dismantling the engine (decarbonizing) which costs ₹3,000-5,000 and takes 2-3 days. Hydrogen Carbon Cleaning removes these deposits chemically without opening a single bolt—in just 45 minutes.

The Science Behind Hydrogen Cleaning

No chemicals. No dismantling. Just pure hydrogen technology that dissolves carbon molecularly.

01. Hydrogen Generation

Our machine uses electrolysis to split water into hydrogen and oxygen (HHO gas). This creates oxyhydrogen—a highly combustible, clean gas.

02. Combustion Process

HHO gas is fed into the engine's air intake. When ignited, it burns at 2000°C—hot enough to vaporize carbon deposits without damaging metal engine parts.

03. Exhaust & Result

Carbon turns into gaseous CO2 and exits through exhaust. What remains is a clean combustion chamber, restored compression, and factory-fresh performance.
